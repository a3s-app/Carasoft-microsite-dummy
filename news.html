Accessibility News

Perspective Tester Launches A3S Platform for Government Accessibility

March 20, 2026

Perspective Tester officially launches A3S (Accessibility as a Service), a comprehensive platform designed to help government agencies achieve and maintain WCAG 2.2 AA compliance. The platform combines automated scanning with expert manual testing by native assistive technology users.

El Paso County Partners with PT for Digital Accessibility Compliance

March 12, 2026

El Paso County selects Perspective Tester to audit and remediate its public-facing digital properties ahead of the ADA Title II compliance deadline. The engagement covers web applications, PDF documents, and mobile interfaces across county departments.

ADA Title II Digital Accessibility Deadline: What You Need to Know

March 5, 2026

With the ADA Title II digital accessibility deadline approaching, state and local government agencies must ensure their websites and digital services meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards. Perspective Tester outlines the key requirements and steps agencies should take to achieve compliance.

Perspective Tester and Carahsoft Announce Government Distribution Partnership

February 20, 2026

Perspective Tester announces a strategic partnership with Carahsoft Technology Corp. to distribute its accessibility testing and compliance solutions to the public sector. The partnership enables government agencies to procure PT services through established contract vehicles.

P15R Platform Adds Bidirectional Jira and Slack Integrations

February 10, 2026

The P15R Accessibility Ops Platform now features bidirectional integrations with Jira and Slack, enabling development teams to manage accessibility issues directly within their existing workflows. Issues discovered during audits automatically sync as tickets with full remediation guidance.

Engineering Equity: Perspective Tester's Vision for Digital Accessibility

January 28, 2026

Perspective Tester CEO Jason McKee shares the company's founding vision of engineering equity for the digital world. The article explores how combining IAAP-certified professionals with native assistive technology users creates a uniquely effective approach to accessibility testing.
